Jollof rice recipe

Description

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made with rice cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce, seasoned with spices like garlic, onions, thyme, and chili peppers. It's often served with meat, fish, or vegetables and is especially popular in countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal. Each region has its own twist on the recipe, making it a beloved and often debated staple.

Ingredients

  • Parboiled long grain rice
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Tomato paste
  • Red bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet
  • Onion
  • Garlic & ginger
  • Vegetable oil
  • Stock (chicken or vegetable)
  • Salt & seasoning cubes
  • Thyme & curry powder
  • Bay leaves

Steps

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, Scotch bonnet, onion, garlic, and ginger into a smooth mix.
  2. Heat oil in a pot and saut√© chopped onions until soft.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for a few minutes to reduce acidity.
  4. Pour in the blended mix and cook until the sauce thickens and oil begins to separate.
  5. Season with salt, seasoning cubes, thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves.
  6. Add stock and bring the sauce to a simmer.
  7. Rinse and add rice to the pot, stirring to combine with the sauce.
  8. Cover tightly and cook on low heat until rice is done, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  9. Optional: Add vegetables or cooked meat/fish in the final minutes and steam until heated through.
  10. Fluff rice with a fork and serve hot.
